The trailhead is easy to miss: a dirt pullout on Bolinas-Fairfax Road near the Azalea Hill sign, where a handful of cars can park along the shoulder on a good day. Arrive by 9 a.m. on a weekend and you'll have the fire road mostly to yourself. The Pine Mountain fire road climbs immediately — no warm-up, just a steady grind through coyote brush and manzanita with views opening west toward the ocean almost at once. Underfoot is packed gravel, wide enough for a maintenance truck, the kind of surface that feels easy until you check the elevation profile later.

The falls themselves are less than a mile in, just off the main road on a short spur. Three distinct drops, each spilling into a dark green plunge pool — the geology here is greenstone, a reminder that this part of the Coast Ranges was once seafloor. When I visited in late April, the flow was strong enough to kick up a fine mist across the overlook deck, a small wooden platform with a single bench. Stewards from the Water District have posted signs asking hikers to stay off the rocks to protect habitat for the California red-legged frog and the yellow-legged frog, both of which breed in the pools below. Take them seriously — the frogs are genuinely here, and the area is actively managed.

After the falls, most hikers turn around, but the fire road continues higher into the Preserve and eventually links to the Pine Mountain ridge if you want a longer day. On the drive back east, consider continuing on Bolinas-Fairfax Road all the way down to Bolinas itself — get a sandwich at the deli on Wharf Road, poke around the town that isn't on the road signs, and add another hour to the outing. The pairing makes the drive worth it.
